Digital Signature

The electronic signature is a set of electronic data that accompany or that are associated with an electronic document and whose core functions are:

  • To identify the signatory unequivocally.
  • To ensure the integrity of the document that was signed.
  • To ensure that the document signed is exactly the same as the original and that it has not suffered alteration or manipulation.

The legal basis of an electronic signature is set out in the law 59/2003 of Electronic Signature and is taking place in more depth in section Legal basis for Signatures. The section also explores, under what circumstances the law equates the electronic signature to a handwritten signature, notes on adds european standards and different legal references to time stamps and advanced.

The Electronic Certificate

The electronic certificate or the electronic identity card contains a cryptographic keys that are the elements necessary for signing. The electronic certificates are intended to identify with certainty the possessor and are issued by Suppliers of certification services.

Process of Electronic Signature

The basic process for the electronic signature is the following one:

  • The user has an electronic document (a spreadsheet, a pdf, an image, even a form in a web page) and a certificate belongs and identifies him.
  • The used digital device for the digital signature summarizes the document.
  • The summary of the document only contains few lines. The summary is unique and any modification of the document also implies a modification of the summary.
  • The implementation of the private key used to encode the summary.
  • The digital device creates another electronic document that contains the encoded summary.
  • The digital device creates another electronic document that contains the encoded document.This new document is the digital signature.
  • The result of this process is an electronic document obtained from the original document and of the keys of the signatory. The electronic signature, it is therefore the same electronic document.
