Digital Signature

The electronic signature is a set of electronic data that accompany or that are associated with an electronic document and whose core functions are:

  • Identify the signatory unequivocally
  • Ensure the integrity of the document that was signed.
  • Ensures that the document signed is exactly the same as the original and that it has not suffered alteration or manipulation

The legal basis of an electronic signature is set out in the law 59/2003 of Electronic Signature and is taking place in more depth in section Legal basis for Signatures . The section also explores, under what circumstances the law equates the electronic signature to a handwritten signature, notes on adds european standards and different legal references to time stamps and advanced.
